I serve as Principal for Worldwide Hardware Reliability, working at the intersection of reliability engineering, statistical modeling, and large-scale AI infrastructure.

My focus is understanding how complex systems fail when uncertainty, scale, and rare events challenge traditional assumptions.

Modern compute-dense platforms increasingly operate in regimes where classical reliability methods are insufficient. Statistical reasoning, probabilistic modeling, and rigorous field data interpretation are foundational to informed engineering leadership.

Reliability is not only about preventing failure. It is about modeling uncertainty clearly and thinking precisely about risk in complex systems.
